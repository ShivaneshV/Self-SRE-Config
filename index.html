Operations Manual: Self.SRE

1. What is this?

If a production server crashes, DevOps pipelines (like GitLab CI/CD) automatically reroute traffic and trigger failovers. But when a human crashes—due to illness, a panic attack, or severe burnout—our daily lives devolve into chaos. Self.SRE applies Enterprise DevOps to human biology. We treat the human as a "Target Node", monitor their telemetry, and use the Google Cloud Agent Builder + GitLab MCP to autonomously execute physical schedule failovers.

2. How to Test The Interactive Demo

Step A: The CI/CD Bio-Linter Navigate to the [02] CI/CD BIO-PIPELINE tab. Click ▶ RERUN BIO-LINTER. Watch as the AI parses your daily schedule exactly like a software linter, failing the pipeline if it detects cognitive overload (e.g., missing your lunch break).
Step B: The Dead Man's Switch Go to the [01] LIVE MISSION CONTROL tab. Click the red ⚠️ INJECT BIO-ANOMALY button in the top right. Do not touch your keyboard. The Gemini Daemon will detect the biometric spike (heart rate/stress), realize you are incapacitated, and trigger an autonomous 5-second countdown.
Step C: The Digital Twin (Auto-Scaling) Watch the failover execute. Low-priority tasks are cancelled. However, for the high-priority Q3 Pitch, the AI overrides the cancellation, pulls your slide deck from the GitLab repo, and spins up an AI Proxy (Digital Twin) to handle the meeting for you.

3. The GitLab Partner Integration

This project was built specifically for the GitLab Partner Track. The AI does not just chat. It executes JSON-RPC tools against the GitLab MCP Server to read the raw schedule.json file, autonomously branch the repo, and utilize GitLab CI/CD concepts to Lint biological schedules (See Tab 2).
