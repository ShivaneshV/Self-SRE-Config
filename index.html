1. Core Concept

When a server fails, SRE systems auto-scale and reroute traffic. When humans fail (burnout, illness), schedules collapse. This project applies Enterprise DevOps to human biology. We store the human schedule as schedule.json in a GitLab repository. The Gemini AI daemon monitors telemetry and uses the GitLab MCP to autonomously execute commits, branches, and merge requests to failover the human's life.

2. Interactive Sandbox Guide

A. The Predictive Bio-Linter

Navigate to the GitLab Bio-Linter tab. Click Run Pipeline. Watch Gemini parse the schedule and fail the build to prevent burnout before the day begins.

B. Reactive Dead Man's Switch

Go to the Live Monitoring tab. Click the Inject Bio-Anomaly button in the header. Do not touch your keyboard. The daemon will detect a heart rate spike, realize you are incapacitated, and trigger an automated Git failover.

C. AI Auto-Scaling (Digital Twin)

During the failover on Tab 1, watch the P1 task (Q3 Pitch). Instead of cancelling it, the AI overrides the failover logic, uses GitLab MCP to pull your slide deck, and dynamically deploys an AI Proxy (Digital Twin) to handle the meeting.
